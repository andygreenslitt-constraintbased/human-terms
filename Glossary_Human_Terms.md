# Glossary: Human Terms

## Purpose

This glossary translates recurring technical phrases into readable terms without
changing their meaning.

Each entry should preserve:

```text
Technical Term
Human-Term Translation
Locked Meaning
Drift Risk
```

## Starter Entries

| Technical Term | Human-Term Translation | Locked Meaning | Drift Risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Object | The thing being worked on | A bounded target must exist before movement | Calling everything the object |
| Constraint | What limits the move | Movement is shaped by limits, not just intent | Treating constraint as a negative instead of a guide |
| Carrier | What carries the move or transfer | A move needs a structure/path that can actually support it | Assuming intention is enough |
| Margin | The weakest point | The move is limited by its lowest support, not its strongest feature | Averaging away the weak point |
| Receipt | A trace that shows what happened | A stage should leave inspectable evidence of its input, output, and authority | Treating a nice answer as a receipt |
| HOLD | Stop because the object/authority/support is missing | No safe movement yet | Treating HOLD as failure |
| REPAIR | Move only by fixing the weak point | Partial support exists, but not enough for closure | Treating repair as permission to proceed fully |
| BLOCK | Stop because a hard boundary was violated | The requested move is not admissible | Softening a real block into a suggestion |
| Human Lyric | Plain-language meaning | Readability layer above the source | Replacing the source |
| Technical Notes | Source structure underneath | The code/equation/claim/document still controls meaning | Paraphrasing too loosely |
| Locked Meaning | What must survive translation | The invariant meaning across explanations | Letting analogy change the claim |
| Audit Layer | Risks and support limits | Translation must expose dependencies and uncertainty | Hiding uncertainty because the explanation sounds good |
